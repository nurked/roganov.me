The Wolf ran through the forest. His heels flashed and branches cut painfully across his muzzle. Never in his life had he run so fast. Behind him lay terror and burning wreckage falling from the sky.

"Run! Lord, if only someone would help! Faster! Run!"

A couple of minutes later the trees parted, and he burst into a clearing.

"LESHYYY!" the Wolf bellowed at the top of his lungs. "Leeeeshyyy!"

"What's happened here? What's going on? Major, what are you doing in the middle of the night? Why are you waking me?"

"Comrade General, disaster! A genuine arctic fox has crept up on us unawares," replied the Wolf, breathing heavily. *(Translator's note: "arctic fox" — polyarniy pesets — is a Russian euphemism for an obscenity meaning total catastrophe.)*

"Oh, Major, that's no way to file a report. Come now, Wolf, pull yourself together — I haven't seen you this frightened in a long while. What's happened? Who's disturbing the peace of the forest?"

"The flying barge! She's been shot down! She tore across the entire firmament, burning brighter than the red sun, and when she slammed into the celestial vault, she came crashing down by the Lake! What a racket it made! The hares all crawled out of their burrows in the middle of the night and started howling..."

"WHAT?" Leshy *(the forest spirit)* took his time processing information, and things reached him slowly. "A flying-shipwreck? Dobrynya Nikitich's barge has crashed?"

"Well, that's... that's what I came running about..."

*(The birch bark breaks off here)*

### Appendix

*Birch-bark document, report of Major Wolf Greyflanks to the General of the Sixth Forest, Leshy*

Date: The third day of this present year.

Location of incident: The ravine beyond the forest edge.

**Event:**

At sunset on the third day, I, Major Wolf Greyflanks, was conducting my standard patrol of the forest with the objective of locating criminal elements and biting the aforesaid in their flanks. Until sunset itself no problems whatsoever were observed in the forest. The hares, as is proper, had finished their round-dances and hidden away in their burrows. The vagrant bears had been expelled from the Gypsy establishments earlier and sent off to sleep in their dens. After sunset I continued running through the forest, until such time as I began to go stiff with cold. This was in the vicinity of the ravine, and I resolved to turn homeward to my den, as all was quiet in the forest.

Immediately after I had turned toward my den, three flashes occurred behind my back. The flashes themselves I did not see, but they lit up the forest brightly and exceedingly. When I turned round to see what was happening, I observed a flying vessel (subsequently identified as the flying barge of Dobrynya Nikitich) that was engulfed in bright flame. Said vessel at full speed struck the celestial vault with such force that stars came showering down. As a result of the collision with the vault, the structural integrity of the vessel's fuselage was compromised, and all the enchanted words that had kept it aloft escaped outward. In consequence whereof the vessel listed and fell on the northern shore of the Forest Lake.

Not knowing what else to do, I ran to General Leshy to report what had occurred. I write this birch bark, as I was in no state to set forth anything in oral form, given the magnitude of the catastrophe. I was administered the finest herbal tinctures of the Water Spirit, and upon coming to my senses composed this document.

I confirm the foregoing as the honest truth, set down the print of my paw, and am of sound health and mental equilibrium.

Wolf Greyflanks.

*(End of birch-bark document)*

### Appendix

*Birch-bark document*

All is quiet in the forest. The sundry little creatures have fallen asleep. Little Hare has fallen asleep too, slumbering in his burrow, snuggled up to his sons and daughters. The Boar sleeps, and the Badger. All sleep — only little Wolf Greyflanks prowls about by night and sees to it that no one crawls out of their burrows, keeping order.

And then — CRACK! And off it went! First everything exploded thrice in the sky, then came a flash of indescribable brightness. And after that the barge struck the celestial vault with such force that stars came showering from the heavens. And then all the enchanted words flew right out of the barge, and down she hurtled from the sky toward Mother Earth and smashed into her! And she was on fire, no less!

And all the living creatures that had been sleeping — everyone who could — came leaping out of their burrows and beheld this horror. At that the Boar reckoned that from such a blaze the whole forest would burn, to the delight of the devils themselves. And off he charged to the Lake, to beat back the flames with clean water.

Other beasts quickly joined the Boar. They even roused the vagrant bears, for the animals knew that the forest could not cope with such a quantity of bright flame on its own.

Everyone worked as one, in concert, and by morning they had extinguished the bright blaze and saved the forest. Only there was nothing left of the barge. Naught but soot and wreckage. How displeased the Water Spirit was with all this, for all that filth was running into the Lake, and from there 'twas not far to his Swamp.

But never mind — by morning the forest was saved, and that was well enough.

*(End of birch-bark document)*

### Appendix

*What the little bird sang*

General Leshy, upon learning what had come to pass, immediately opened an investigation into the incident. The Wolf reported on the matter promptly and efficiently, but the data was scant.

After interviews with sundry forest creatures the following was established:

Dobrynya Nikitich's barge had been flying over the forest for a fortnight already. According to unverified rumours, Dobrynya had been seeking refuge from Koschei the Immortal, who had taken a disliking to Dobrynya. But these rumours were chirped on the Chirper *(i.e. Twitter)* by the Sparrows, and one cannot believe them at all.

The Sow, however, related much that was useful. A few days ago she and the Boar had exhausted their winter stores, and she had been obliged to go to the edge of the forest to dig up mushrooms they had cached back in autumn. As she was returning home, just before sunset, she spotted Zmey Gorynych *(the three-headed dragon)*, who was flying in the direction of the Lake. The Serpent flew very low — so low that none of Vasilisa the Wise's detection charms could latch onto him, and by all appearances the Sorcerer-Oaks that operated the Unidentified Flying Creature detection system were unable to detect Gorynych either.

The Sow gave testimony in proper form, on the basis of which the case was referred to the General Prosecutor's Office, which, as it happened, then summoned Gorynych himself for an explanation.

*(End of song, published on the Chirper, the fifth day of this present year)*

### Appendix

*Reflections of Leshy, as carved by his own hand upon birch bark*

After the interrogation of Gorynych, I, as General of the Forest, was compelled to revisit my theories and get to the bottom of things. It transpired that the Sow had simply been bought off with potatoes and made to bear false witness. In truth the Sow had been lounging about with the Boar all night until the barge crashed and had gone nowhere. The Sorcerer-Oaks had known about the barge and been tracking it from the moment of its appearance — Vasilisa's enchantments worked on short words and their resolution was high, sufficient to identify the barge's model.

The investigation stalled for several weeks, as the Sow swore up and down that she did not know who had paid her, and all communication had been conducted exclusively via the Chirper.

But after a pood of potatoes was delivered to the Sow directly in gaol — and not by any ordinary means, but by the Little Humpbacked Horse — things began to unravel of their own accord. The Horse was detained, but upon investigation it turned out he himself knew nothing. However, since he worked for the most prestigious delivery company in the entire Tsardom, tracing the client was no difficult matter. Fortunately, the peasant's son was not averse to helping sort things out.

The order had been placed on behalf of Vasilisa the Wise.

Well now — there's a pretty pickle. What on earth does this mean?

"Me? What have I to do with it?"

*(The birch bark is cut short)*

### Appendix

*Retelling of the chronicle of Dobrynya Nikitich, as recorded on animated birch bark in his video-chronicle, owing to you wretches lacking the codecs to play animated birch bark*

A pox upon those foreign villains! We are the strength of this land! Without our mechanical enchantments progress shall not advance a single step. The Tsardom needs peace, and only proper mechanised equipment can ensure it.

Take, for instance, Ivan the Fool. He was a fool of fools. The self-taught engineer hit upon the idea of mounting a stove on caterpillar tracks — and mount it he did! And then what did he do? Running on firewood it went too slowly for his liking. And smoked something dreadful. So he found some gemstones (the kind that glow green in the dark) and stuffed them into the stove, and when they cracked — that gave him speed aplenty. 'Twas a good thing he fell off the stove before he could get up to full speed. The moment the stove hit a hillock, off it flew so far that even the devils couldn't find it afterwards. Word is it either smashed into the celestial vault or toppled off the edge of the earth!

A fool he may be, but then we arrived — Dobrynya and his retinue. We beheld a bright light in the stove's maw when he began tossing those gemstones in, and we marvelled at the mighty steam that billowed forth and how fast it drove the running gear.

And so our chief engineers thought, and deliberated, and conjured, and devised a contraption of enchanted words which, by the method of our native birch-bark assembly language, packed energy into quanta and governed those quanta so that the stove became controllable. After recalibration of the control mechanism, the stove was more obedient than an old mare! It went backwards and forwards. Slow and fast. One could mount a cannon upon it and launch cannonballs with quanta — in military affairs such a contraption would be beyond price.

But there is one thing ye must know. The whole affair took us three months. Two of our finest mind-warriors *(i.e. programmers)* worked three months, writing and uttering enchanted words in birch-bark assembly. We approach each one individually, and therefore the birch-assembly coders in our Tsardom are of the first order.

But a terrible peril threatens us all.

Foreigners have descended upon our lands and set about luring our little ones into their unnatural camp.

I say unto you: to learn birch-bark programming takes years. One must serve as an apprentice and read birch-bark treatises five or six times a year. One must study and practise and look upon systems of every kind. One must know sundry languages and understand how enchanted words work, and how an enchantment is executed in birch-bark assembly.

But these foreigners entice our young with quick lessons on video-chronicles, and show them how to work on foreign self-serving systems *(i.e. high-level frameworks)* that write the code and do the conjuring for them. Their code is gathered into chest-repositories *(i.e. npm packages)*, and instead of teaching you to write your own code, they train you never to write any code at all and simply to pull it from the chests. And the chests are enchanted! Whatever you take from them appears on its own, so you may take as much as your heart desires.

And what then? I have seen these pseudo-mind-warriors. What rubbish they write! Someone tried to install so many enchanted words on that stove of Ivan's that the stove nearly cracked clean in twain. A disgrace.

I know, good people, this is no accident. It is a universal conspiracy to destroy our Tsardom. And I shall not sit idle! This cannot be allowed, for a foreign invasion will come and crush all our bright minds.

Therefore I declare war upon these foreign incursions. We managed to brew gold in the self-boiling cauldron that young Ivan assembled from his gemstones. The gold came out fine, of high assay. With this gold I purchased a great flying vessel, and we programmed her as was needful.

Upon this vessel I mounted a magic-radiating mirror, which creates a pulse and fires upon the foreign invaders with such radiation that their self-inscribing chests disintegrate into dust and vanish. I shall ascend high above the forest — so high they cannot find me — and fire upon all those chests with pulses, defending our Tsardom from invasion.

We shall preserve our traditions and our true mind-warriors, who have always been the pride of our Tsardom.

Hold fast! I am with you.

Dobrynya.

*(End of retelling)*

### Appendix

*Birch bark from the personal diary of Vasilisa the Wise, recorded by her firebird-scribe*

"No, Investigator Leshy, hear me out! Have you any idea what this Dobrynya has wrought? Some champion of intellectual purity and propagandist of assembly language he is. And how am I supposed to live after this? And Yaga?"

"Well, what's Yaga got to do with it?"

"Oh, the things I must explain to you lot... Yes, I understand — nowadays Baba Yaga is just a decrepit old hag to you. But think for a moment: how did she manage to build her underground network of ferromagnets that sustains the levitation of her mortar? She was quite the beauty, once upon a time. Only it was long ago, in days of old. She assembled a retinue of suitors, went about, took her pick. Once she married, she invested her capital wisely. And what investments she made — oh my! She had the good fortune of living a thousand years, and her husbands tended to die off quickly. Whenever one passed on, a new one came along. The woman preserved herself well. Don't get me wrong, Leshy — she never laid a finger on anyone. But at sixty, any bogatyr is apt to keel over. She would grieve a year or two, and then a new dashing fellow would appear."

"And so?"

"And so — that is how she amassed her fortune. That is how she helped our Tsardom. First she installed ferromagnets everywhere. Thanks to these, her mortar, with the right software, gained the ability to fly through the air. And when her youth ran out, and she got her bone leg — well, how can you deny a cripple her means of transportation? So now she flies."

"Right, so she did all that — and? It was all for herself!"

"Oh, Leshy! Go to... the devils. What do you mean, for herself? Don't you know that it was on the basis of her system that my company developed far more powerful flying vessels? Do you think they'd be flying about here without me? Hmm?"

"Well..."

"Well indeed! Then along I come, and what do I see? A woman of years, holding a fortune, investing it in the advancement of science. So I joined in. Beauty was always mine, and I did not become 'the Wise' for nothing. Seeing the potential for development, I found myself a wealthy foreign husband. We had a quick wedding, and when the Sorcerer-Oaks went to ICO, I played the market successfully and multiplied his foreign capital many times over. And do you think I stopped there? Not a bit of it! I saw potential in Yaga's business. Who do you think invested in the system of fireflies that we fashioned to look like stars and nailed to the celestial vault *(i.e. GPS satellites)*? There. That all came from my coffers. And without that system — what?"

"What?"

"Nothing! You'd have nothing at all! The Seven-Petalled Flower doesn't work. The Hut on Chicken Legs — how do you think it tells front from back? By those very fireflies! My Magic Mirror — how do you think it computes who is where? By the very same!"

"Right, well, what's Dobrynya got to do with it then? What did he do to you?"

"Leshy, honestly! With his foreigner-hatred he ruined everything for Yaga and me! Started firing his beams left and right. And Yaga herself — half the code in her mortar comes from the foreigners' chests! She dropped right out of the sky. He nearly killed the old dear! And that would have been bad enough — I could have re-enchanted her mortar myself in birch-bark assembly. But he never tested his mirror. Never even worked out where all that output power was coming from, or what those gemstones feeding the mirror actually were. The radio wave from the mirror didn't propagate as a beam, but as a burst that radiated in all directions. When that burst hit the celestial vault, half the fireflies came raining down from the sky. We had so many problems you couldn't begin to count them. The Hut on Chicken Legs spun round for two days until it found enough fireflies for positioning. And all those conspirators we'd sold mirrors to very nearly crashed our support service. 'We can't surveil our brides, can't figure out who's going where' — you see the sort of thing."

"Well, so Dobrynya threatened you — but your business is legitimate. You could have appealed to the Prince for help."

"Well, that's just what we did. But the Prince believes none of it and won't hear a word — not about Dobrynya, not about Yaga. He wants facts, if you please. So we had to take matters into our own hands."

"And what did you take?"

"Well, Yaga and I came to an arrangement. She went to Koschei and brought him gemstones. He enchanted them properly and fashioned photon torpedoes — the latest in foreign anti-aircraft technology. As soon as everyone had turned away, under cover of night we launched three of those torpedoes at Nikitich's barge, to knock her out good and proper."

"Good Lord above — and the people? What happened to the people?"

"Leshy, heavens — you'd do better conducting investigations than wasting time on trifles. We sent a drill-alarm signal. They all evacuated the vessel fifteen minutes before the torpedoes hit. While they were milling about outside the barge, we blasted her."

"But how... I never..."

"Of course you didn't know! Naturally! They're conspiracy-minded lot through and through. The moment they saw their barge in wreckage, they bolted as far away as they could. Off to hide and find a new refuge."

"That is to say — everyone is alive?"

"Of course. I can even give you the coordinates. The fireflies have settled down; the positioning system is working again."

"Well, and now what?"

"What? My dear husband kicked the bucket some two years ago. The other day I married Ivan the Fool. And he controls all the gemstone mines. And believe me — I will not let Nikitich anywhere near them. So he'll have nothing with which to disrupt the systems of the Tsardom. Let him sit tight and conspire to his heart's content!"

"Oh, you cunning thing, Vasilisa! But what about the damages?"

"What damages? How did he get the money? Transmuted iron into gold. What's going to happen now if anyone who fancies it starts transmuting iron into gold? We won't know where to put the stuff. We now have a hole in the budget of ten poods of gold backed by nothing. Hmm? Let the treasury scribes in the royal city sort out what he's done — bubble or gold. And I, having burned it all, have returned matters to their rightful state. No more problems."

"Oh, Vasilisa, oh you..."

"Leshy, you couldn't bring charges against me if you tried. Koschei did it all, and he, as is well known, may plot as many schemes as he likes. So come along, Leshy, let's wrap this up."

*(End of birch bark)*

### Decree

*Scroll-decree from the Royal City of the Tsardom*

Having considered all aspects of the matter of Dobrynya Nikitich's barge, the Tsar-Sovereign has issued the following decree, taking effect forthwith and in every corner of the Tsardom.

This whole rigmarole has gotten completely out of hand. After all these altercations between your Gorynychs, your Nikitiches and your Yagas, a man cannot sleep in peace in this Realm. Ships fall from the sky. Technologies we spent years putting into service — for which you all vouched and told me how everything would work for centuries — all of it is falling from the firmament and none of it works. There is no peace; there is nowhere to raise children! Therefore I decree:

1. All ferromagnets shall be dug from the earth and sold to the foreigners.
2. All the fireflies shall be shot down from the sky.
3. All flying vessels shall be disenchanted and set upon the water! Nothing without wings shall fly.
4. Ivan — all gemstones are to be buried and you shall forget where you found them. The stove is to be disassembled and reassembled in a normal, stationary condition, without gemstones! If anyone learns where to obtain these gemstones — 'twill cost you your head.
5. Dobrynya is to take himself in hand forthwith and begin training me a retinue! Such a retinue as can defend against foreigners! Every one of your warriors must be able to carry two cows upon his back without strain. If I see you with a belly a year hence — 'twill cost you your head.
6. Yaga — remove yourself. Go to Koschei if you will, or into the thickets, but if I see you on the road — 'twill cost you your head.
7. Vasilisa — either you come to your senses and take up spinning thread and teaching wisdom to children, or 'twill cost you your head as well. Sit quietly and keep your head down. If I see you in business one more time — you know what I'll do.
8. Leshy and the Water Spirit — sit quietly and keep your heads down. You don't even need to stick them up! Live out your years; your pensions are paid; sit nice and quiet!
9. Gorynych — you are ostensibly uninvolved, but I know you. I tell you kindly: fly to the Southern Lands and hide there. If I find you — 'twill cost you your heads. And don't you dare show off — I still have the armies of Popovich and Muromets, and it'll be no laughing matter; we'll chop off everything there is to chop.
10. To all the forest folk, including boars, wolves, and hares — there is no need for you to be whispering with forest spirits. We humans do not understand you, and all who can translate are removing themselves. Therefore I recommend sitting quietly, behaving as is proper, and making no attempts at contact. And if I catch you at it — 'twill cost you your heads, and into the soup you go!

The decree takes effect immediately upon reading.

Vladimir the Bright Sun.

*(End of decree)*
